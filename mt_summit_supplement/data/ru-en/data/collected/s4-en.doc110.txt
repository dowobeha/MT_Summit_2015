Syria destroyed chemical weapon equipment
The organization for the prohibition of chemical weaponsthe recognized that Syria destroyed all the declared facilities of chemical weapons production.
Damascus finished the first step of diarmament the day before the deadline .
BBC reports that Syria had to destroy the manufacturing and other auxiliary objects, that are intended for preparation of toxic substances, by 1st of november.
The destruction of the stockpile of toxic substances or their chemical components should be completed by the middle of 2014 .
According to the representative of the opcw , currently toxic substances are located in locked repositories .
International inspectors went to Syria after the chemical attack on the Damascus outskirts were according to the US more than 1400 people got killed .
The main task of the inspectors was to stop Syrian authorities from producing chemical weapon and to destroy  mixing chemicals and munitions infrastructure .
Now these manufacturing facilities will be under careful watch for preventing their repair .
The next milestone is the middle of november : by this time the opcw and syria must agree to a detailed plan of destruction of existing stocks of toxic substances .

The united states believed that the shelling was held by regular troops .
Syria and Russia insist that this was the work of the syrian rebels .
 In accordance with the agreement, reached by Russian mediation, the world community demanded from the syrian leadership to destroy or lead in inoperable all capacity for the production of shells , toxic gases, liquids and shells for filling in these substances .
The next step must be the destruction of chemical weapon that Syrian army has .
It is assumed that this could be arsenal with more than 1,000 tons of toxic substances кожно-нарывного__kozhno-naryvnogo нервно-паралитических__nervno-paraliticheskikh , mustard gas and other banned chemicals stored in several dozen different places .
For its destruction it is necessary that opcw and the Syrian authorities for the agreed timetable of action - it is two weeks .
The head of the opcw field operations Jerry Smith told the BBC that his inspectors " personally watched the destruction of the equipment ."
“ Now they have no opportunity to conduct further production, or mixing chemical weapons , ” he said .
In the statement , the opcw said its inspectors visited 21 of the 23 sites in Syria and convinced that factories for manufacturing  shells and toxic substances , either destroyed or are in order .
The documents said that the remaining two objects are too dangerous to carry out an inspection , but the equipment was carried out and inspected by specialists of the opcw .
According to Smith , the process of destruction the chemical weapon was  " very difficult" because they had to work in the ongoing conflict .
In early october , the opcw was awarded the nobel peace prize . However , according to Smith , the inspectors did not have time to celebrate it - they were too busy with work .
